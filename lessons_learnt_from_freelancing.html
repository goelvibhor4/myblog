Lessons learnt from freelancing

Posted by Vibhor Goel on October 7, 2015

I’ve been working as a freelance web developer for almost two years now. Being both a freelancer and a college student has taught me a lot of things about work life balance. These years I've had the chance to test my capacities and network with wide range of individuals .


Here’s a few of the lessons I’ve learned so far :

1. Focus on learning, Money will follow.

I started building sites for friends of mine, charged them next to nothing. There were days when I didn't charged a single penny for website and now are days when some clients are paying me nearly 50k per website. Learning from each project has ensured that my career continues to flourish and has helped me to grow both as a professional freelancer and as a person.

2. Don’t worry too much about CGPA at college.

Last week while I was going through my past semester results, I realized I was amongst the top ten students in my stream at college for the first semester. Never was I again seen in top ten students list in next years. I still hold a decent position as far as academics is concerned, but more importantly what I learnt is life is NOT ONLY about grades, ranks, colleges. It's about experience, and doing something you love!.

3. Don't say yes to everything.

In case you're new to freelancing,then you may be enticed to jump at any and every project that is offered to you. In any case, some work is simply not worth having. Don't be afraid to say NO to a new project if you are already occupied with many.

4. Freelancing is more about confidence rather than experience.

Most of the projects I have worked on were first timers. Sometimes while taking the project, I myself didn’t knew how to do it. So, as a freelancer, nobody is going to go to bat for you except yourself. If you believe in yourself, you can do wonders.


There will be many situations where you are going to listen "No", have customers vanish(Has happened with me many times) and quote individuals who can't bear the cost of you. But this should not stop your business.
